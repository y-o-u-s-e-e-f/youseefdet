01 About
cat about.txt

I’m Yusuf, a junior studying Electrical Engineering at the University of Michigan, Ann Arbor. I transferred from Henry Ford Community College and haven’t slowed down since.

What keeps me engaged is the bridge between hardware and software. In EECS 215 I’m getting into the real fundamentals — op-amps, transistors, RLC circuits — while EECS 280 is pushing me to think seriously about how to write clean, well-structured C++. EECS 201 got me comfortable actually using a terminal — Makefiles, version control, debugging tools — the stuff you don’t learn in a textbook. And in EECS 200, I’m building a rescue robot that’s supposed to navigate a building and detect heat signatures. Every week we add something new to it.

I’m still figuring out exactly where in EE I want to go deep — I’d rather be honest about that than fake a specialty. What I do know is I like the point where a circuit starts responding to code. I’m looking for an internship where I can do real work and keep learning fast.
